KIN Robot

The KIN Robot (short for "kinematics") was designed to teach members of the American Society of Mechanical Engineers (ASME) about robotics. The workshop includes, but is not limited to basic motor control, basic circuitry, programming, and concepts of forward and inverse kinematics in robotics. Students control the robot using coordinate input or joysticks. Since each workshop enrolls 15 members on average, the KIN Robot was designed to be affordable, consisting of very few parts.

Mechanical Design

The KIN Robot is laser cut out of acrylic, only taking up 23 X 23 cm. Acrylic was chosen because it is readily available and easy to cut with a laser cnc machine. In addition, acrylic is smooth compared to other materials, resulting in low friction when parts are in contact. Once the parts are cut, the robot is assembled using select nuts and bolts. The robot is driven by small hobby servo motors and controlled by an Arduino microcontroller. The overall assembly requires very little wiring since a lot of the components like the servos have integrated circuits.
